General Child Safety Guidelines

❌
Caffeine — Strictly avoid for children under 12. Present in sodas, energy drinks, and chocolate-flavored products.
⚠️
Artificial Colors (Red 40, Yellow 5, Blue 1) — Linked to hyperactivity and ADHD symptoms. EU requires warning labels on all products containing these dyes.
❌
Sodium Nitrate — Found in hot dogs, bacon, deli meats. Should be strictly limited in children's diets due to cancer risk.
⚠️
Excess Added Sugar — Children should consume less than 25g of added sugar per day (WHO). Most packaged snacks exceed this in one serving.
⚠️
Aspartame & Ace-K — Artificial sweeteners not recommended for young children due to unknown long-term developmental effects.
❌
Polysorbate 80 & CMC — May damage the gut lining. Developing immune systems in children are especially vulnerable to microbiome disruption.

Evidence-Based Nutrition

Science-backed diet
principles to live by

🥦

Eat the Rainbow

Aim for 5+ servings of colorful fruits and vegetables daily. Different colors provide different phytonutrients and antioxidants that work synergistically.

🐟

Omega-3 Rich Foods

Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), chia seeds, and walnuts reduce systemic inflammation. WHO recommends at least 2 fish servings per week.

🫘

High-Fibre Diet

25–38g of dietary fibre per day from whole grains, legumes, and vegetables feeds healthy gut bacteria and reduces colorectal cancer risk by up to 40%.

💧

Hydration First

Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily. Proper hydration improves kidney function, energy, cognitive performance, and the body's ability to remove toxins.

⏰

Time-Restricted Eating

A 12–16 hour eating window supports cellular autophagy and insulin sensitivity reset. Consult your doctor before starting any fasting protocol.

🧘

Mindful Eating

Eat slowly, without screens. It takes 20 minutes for satiety signals to reach your brain — rushing meals is one of the leading causes of overeating.
